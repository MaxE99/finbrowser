About

Background

I remember very well the time I started investing. I was absolutely clueless where to get good information on individual companies and the best thing that came to my mind right away was to look up all the YouTube stock research I could find on a company.

However, it didn't take long for me to realize that very few channels conveyed any real informational value. Instead, you mostly only learned a few basic things about the company and heard some metrics.

So I moved on pretty quickly and looked for new sources that go into a little more detail. After finding a number of sites with similarly weak content to YouTube, I found SeekingAlpha. There, for the first time, I came across a wide range of top authors who publish great deep dives. So, over the course of the year, I spent day after day reading through article after article, building a deeper understanding of individual companies and industries.

At the time I was doing this, I noticed that many authors were starting to publish articles on Substack as well. That's how I became aware of the growing number of great new writers on Substack, and my list of sources outside of SeekingAlpha grew rapidly. On Substack, in turn, I became aware that a large community of professional investors had formed on Twitter that shared their knowledge with the masses.

Why I Created This Site

Now, as retail investors, we are very fortunate that such a huge number of great sources exist on the open internet. Unfortunately, these sources are very scattered and it is not always easy to find good information in the large sea of mediocre content. Furthermore, many are not even aware that this content is freely available on the Internet. My hope is to change that with this website and to generate more traffic for authors who generously share their detailed knowledge with us retail investors. I hope that the number of such sources will continue to increase in the future and believe that this could make a great contribution to the democratization of the financial world by reducing the information dissymmetry between Wall Street and Main Street.

About Me

Since I started dealing with stocks more than half a decade ago, it has developed into my biggest hobby, into which a large part of my free time goes. My focus is primarily on the tech industry, although lately I have also been increasingly involved with other industries such as energy and commodities. My other great passion in life is computer science, in which I am currently working on my master's degree. So this website was also a great opportunity to combine my two interests.
